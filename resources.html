Nathan Yau's bubble chart

The visualisation I have chosen to make consists of a bubble chart. The bubble chart is an adapted version of Nathan Yau's bubble chart of The Stages of a Relationship. The reason I chose to use this form of graph to represent the data, was because it allowed categorization by location in a smooth way and could be used to illustrate time passing at the same time.

The national life tables

The National Life Tables contain data about mortality rates in England and Wales. By using the Kannisto-Thatcher methodology to quantify the number of years a person is statistically expected to have left, based on their age, gender and rates of mortality among this group. Of course a drawback of the resultant life expectancy is that it does not reflect the reality for those living with a disability. The tables have been used for future planning in a range of different industries, especially welfare.

The Office for National statistics - interactive

The Office for National Statistics who published the tables, have used them to create an interacting visualisation which takes your age and gender as input and produces a graph showing the likelihood of a person with the specified characteristics reaching 100, while displaying the estimated life expectancy.
